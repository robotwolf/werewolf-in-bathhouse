# Werewolf Bathhouse

## World Outside The Bathhouse

### Purpose
This document defines the larger social world around the bathhouse so the setting feels specific, systemic, and reusable.

It is meant to support design, writing, level dressing, procedural mystery content, and Unreal implementation planning.

This document also clarifies an important direction change:

**Lycanthropy is not the central secret of the setting.**

Werewolves, goblins, spirits, witches, and other magical peoples are normal parts of civic life. The mystery comes from liability, secrecy, bad judgment, and institutional pressure, not from the mere existence of monsters.

### Core Setting Frame
The game takes place on the ragged outer edge of a wealthy magical city whose official self-image is refined, regulated, and cosmopolitan.

The bathhouse exists in the service belt just outside that polished center:

- old utility tunnels
- cheap rentals
- municipal overflow infrastructure
- freight yards for enchanted goods
- nightlife that survives because inspectors do not stay late
- semi-legal businesses that serve workers, creatures, and magical transients no respectable district wants to see

This gives the project its main contrast:

- the city presents itself as elegant and enlightened
- the outskirts run on neglect, compromise, and practical coexistence

The bathhouse is not hidden from the world. It is embedded in a city that tolerates strange things as long as paperwork, insurance, and public scandal remain under control.

### Social Rules Of The Setting
The setting should follow a few stable rules.

#### 1. Supernatural People Are Ordinary Citizens
Species identity is socially meaningful, but not an automatic twist.

- werewolves are known
- vampires are known
- goblins are known
- resident spirits are known
- glamour, warding, and low-grade sorcery are mundane utilities

People may still be prejudiced, flirt with stereotypes, or use species as an excuse. But the world itself should not behave as if every werewolf is a hidden cryptid.

#### 2. Institutions Matter More Than Myth
The city runs on permits, inspectors, guilds, contracts, neighborhood favors, and grudges.

Practical power comes from:

- who owns the property
- who issued the license
- who insures the premises
- who can call in municipal attention
- who can suppress a scandal
- who is owed money
- who has dirt on whom

This keeps the tone grounded. Characters do not only fear claws and curses. They fear hearings, closures, debt, blackmail, and being publicly linked to the wrong idiot.

#### 3. Magic Is Infrastructural
Magic should feel like plumbing, electrical service, bureaucracy, and pest control got drunk together and founded a city.

Examples:

- badge sprites validate staff access and complain when underfed
- goblin service boxes handle parcel exchange, maintenance requests, and bribes
- enchanted boilers regulate steam pressure with temperamental semi-sentience
- municipal wards act like zoning law crossed with ritual geometry
- glamour screens cover ugly repairs until they fail at inconvenient moments
- bound spirits handle repetitive building functions in exchange for upkeep, offerings, or contract clauses

This is where the setting gets most of its texture. Fantasy should rarely feel remote or ancient. It should feel installed, patched, leased, and overdue for service.

#### 4. Respectability Is A Performance
The wealthy city center uses taste, etiquette, and law to hide how much of its comfort depends on ugly labor at the edges.

That means:

- expensive districts outsource magical risk
- dangerous species are tolerated when profitable
- dirty infrastructure is hidden in poor neighborhoods
- scandal matters more than justice
- reform language is often cosmetic

This supports the game's comedy and menace at once. Everyone claims to be civilized. Everything important is held together by exhausted weirdos and suspect contracts.

### The City In Broad Strokes
The player does not need a full atlas, but the world should imply a city with a few clear zones.

#### The Bright Core
The moneyed center of the city.

Traits:

- polished magical transit
- expensive bath clubs and private spas
- strict public conduct expectations
- curated multicultural supernatural chic
- heavy legal shielding

The core buys discretion and exports mess.

#### The Service Ring
The working districts between wealth and the fringe.

Traits:

- laundries
- supply depots
- repair arcades
- wholesale alchemy
- cheap canteens
- worker housing
- strip-mall occultism

This is where the bathhouse sits best: close enough to serve the city, low-status enough to absorb its filth.

#### The Ragged Perimeter
The edge where law, old ruins, and unofficial economies overlap.

Traits:

- abandoned shrines
- freight lots
- improvised night markets
- debt dens
- pack bars
- unlicensed healers
- collapsed infrastructure with new uses layered on top

This zone gives the world scale and a sense that the bathhouse is only one pressure point in a much larger urban organism.

### Important Institutions
These are the forces that make the world feel political and procedural rather than purely folkloric.

#### Municipal License Office
Controls business permits, hazard compliance, occupancy categories, and creature-safety requirements.

Design use:

- creates stakes without direct violence
- explains why management fears paperwork as much as bloodshed
- provides language for legal tags, clue categories, and escalation triggers

#### Magical Risk Insurers
Insurance is one of the setting's real monsters.

They care about:

- transformation incidents
- disappearances
- cursed water contamination
- property damage
- negligent ward maintenance
- unreported injuries

Design use:

- supports scenarios like fraud, coverups, and cooked incident logs
- gives NPCs reasons to lie without turning them into cartoon villains

#### Pack Structures
Werewolf society should be present, but not turned into mystical gibberish for its own sake.

Pack life is a mix of kinship, obligation, hierarchy, reputation, and mutual protection. Some packs are disciplined. Some are loose social gangs. Some are basically family businesses with worse tempers.

The important point:

The rogue werewolf is not dangerous because he is a werewolf. He is dangerous because he is politically attached, socially entangled, and too stupid or proud to stay under control.

#### Labor And Service Networks
Bathhouses rely on laundries, cleaners, alchemists, suppliers, temp workers, and fixers.

Design use:

- expands the clue economy beyond patrons
- supports item flavor, overheard gossip, invoices, delivery records, and service notes
- helps the bathhouse feel like one node in a living economy

#### Informal Criminal Economy
Not a full underworld opera. Just enough rot to make the city honest about itself.

This includes:

- stolen conveyances
- black-market glamours
- potion resale
- insurance fiddling
- forged credentials
- unlicensed healing
- debt collection disguised as social visiting

This is fertile soil for procedural secrets because it is mundane enough to be believable and dirty enough to create fear.

### Species And Social Texture
Species should shape behavior and assumptions, but not flatten characters into hats.

Good setting use:

- species changes legal treatment, social reading, and practical needs
- species can alter access requirements, room suitability, or clue interpretation
- species can inform subculture, not destiny

Bad setting use:

- every species becomes a monoculture
- every werewolf clue points to violence
- every goblin character becomes comic relief
- every supernatural detail requires exposition

For implementation, species should mostly act as profile data that influences:

- tags
- allowed interactions
- room preferences
- stress responses
- rumor interpretation
- clue weighting

### Tone Rules For The Wider World
The world should feel:

- lived-in instead of epic
- urban instead of pastoral
- regulated instead of purely occult
- funny by implication instead of winky parody
- adult, grubby, and socially observant

Good reference energy:

- bad landlords with magical concessions
- city paperwork for impossible problems
- expensive districts using cheap districts as shock absorbers
- bizarre infrastructure treated as routine

The comedy should come from institutional absurdity and human behavior, not from random nonsense.

### Worldbuilding Hooks For Art And Audio
The larger world can show up through small things:

- municipal posters about steam contamination or transformation etiquette
- locker stickers from rival bath clubs
- branded towels from a failed luxury chain
- insurance warning placards
- transit cards with ward burns around the edges
- public-service announcements about moonrise conduct
- half-broken badge sprites muttering policy text

This lets the outside world bleed into the bathhouse without requiring giant exterior levels.

### Procedural Mystery Implications
The wider world should feed the social mystery system directly.

Scenario templates can draw from city institutions:

- insurance scam gone bad
- stolen city conveyance
- pack discipline dispute
- municipal inspector bribery
- black-market potion side business
- labor theft through fake service requests

Secret pools can draw from recognizable urban vices:

- debt
- coverups
- bad hookups
- forged records
- quietly paid settlements
- favors owed to the wrong people

This keeps procedural generation coherent because every lie belongs to a city system instead of floating free in abstract drama soup.

### Hard Rules To Keep
- Do not frame the world as secretly full of monsters. Everyone already knows that.
- Do not make species identity the only source of suspicion.
- Do not make the city center morally cleaner than the fringe. It is simply richer and better insulated.
- Do not explain every magical system in full. Use selective detail and recurring logic.
- Do not let lore outgrow the prototype. The world exists to sharpen play, not to become a wiki infestation.

### Immediate Use In The Prototype
For the vertical slice, the outside world only needs to do four jobs:

1. explain why the bathhouse matters
2. explain why multiple NPCs have plausible dirty ties to one person
3. explain why the Boss fears scandal, licensing, and liability
4. provide a believable source for clues, props, and authored secrets

If it does that, the setting is pulling its weight instead of just posing dramatically in wet lighting.
