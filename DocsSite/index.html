Who Does What Around Here?

This local site is the quick-entry map for the bathhouse stack: who owns layout, who builds shells, who publishes room truth, and who commits runtime management crimes after the lights come up.

Concise Definition

Staging is the semantic query and handoff layer that exposes constructed space as usable room truth for tools, gameplay, and runtime orchestration.

What That Means

When another system needs to find a room, marker, connector, or tagged affordance without digging through geometry like a little goblin surveyor, it should go through Staging.

What It Does Not Mean

Staging does not decide topology. It does not build shells. It does not run Gideon. It explains what already exists and makes that meaning queryable.

The Handoff Chain

Ginny

Chooses what rooms exist, how they connect, what the required spine looks like, and how strange the hall approaches are allowed to get.

Mason

Builds stock or parametric shells when a room wants them. For hero-authored rooms, he can remain attached and politely do nothing.

Staging

Publishes the room contract: markers, connectors, labels, room-selection helpers, probes, and the query surface other systems rely on instead of geometry archaeology.

Gideon

Consumes published room truth at runtime: arrivals, admission, queueing, POIs, crowd flow, and all the management-adjacent nonsense.
