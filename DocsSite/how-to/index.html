Do The Thing Without Guessing

These are the fast routes: make a room, wire it honestly, mark it for gameplay, and test it without summoning a second crisis.

Make A Room

1. Start From A Room Blueprint

Use an existing room as your base. Keep the bounds, connectors, Staging markers, and authored content root intact even if Mason is disabled.

2. Decide Who Builds It

If it is a hero room, disable StockAssemblySettings.bEnabled and ParametricSettings.bEnabled. Mason stays attached, but stops wallpapering over your sky.

3. Keep Truth Honest

Your room can lie visually, but not topologically. Bounds, connectors, room tags, activity tags, and marker families still have to tell the truth.

Add Connectors And Openings

Place connectors where a traversal contract is actually true. Do not let a fake facade trick you into putting the hall connector where the hallway is not.

Add Staging Markers

Marker Families

NPC_*, Task_*, Clue_*, MissionSocket_*, and FX_* are the standard families. Put them on real affordances, not vibes.

Why Staging Exists

Other systems should be able to ask “what can this room support?” without scraping geometry. That is the whole point of the layer.

Editor Readability

The billboard labels are there to keep marker and connector truth readable in-editor without forcing you to nose-kiss the drywall.
