# Werewolf Bathhouse

## Ending Concept: Alpha Intervention

### Purpose
This document captures an early ending concept for the "correct identification" route.

It is not final script. It is a structural note meant to preserve the tone, reveal, and gameplay logic of the ending before the broader GDD is updated.

### Core Intent
The ending should confirm the project's larger truth:

- the problem was never "werewolves exist"
- the problem was one specific reckless werewolf creating legal, social, and business liability
- the Boss has real authority in this world, including sanctioned supernatural authority
- the player's value comes from correctly reading the social mess and surviving long enough to hand it off

This ending should make the player feel they solved the correct problem, but also discovered they now work inside a much larger and uglier system than they first understood.

### Trigger Conditions
This route occurs when:

1. the player correctly identifies Ronin as the central liability
2. the player informs the Boss
3. the player remains alive long enough for the Boss to arrive

The player should understand that this is not a clean "you win instantly" button. Correct deduction creates the endgame confrontation.

### Ending Structure

#### 1. Correct Call
The player calls the Boss and reports the correct identity.

The Boss does not explain much. He is already moving.

The important feeling:

- the player finally has certainty
- certainty immediately creates danger

#### 2. Ronin Realizes He Has Been Sold Out
Ronin figures out that the player has identified him and contacted the Boss.

He is not a theatrical mastermind. He is drunk, angry, humiliated, and dangerous.

His hostility toward the player should feel personal, even if the exact reason is small, petty, or partially imagined.

This should support a short pursuit / pressure sequence inside the bathhouse.

#### 3. Boss Call During Pursuit
At some point during the interior endgame, the Boss calls again.

He is outside.

The player's new objective becomes:

**Lead Ronin outside.**

This reframes the ending from pure survival into controlled baiting, routing, and escape under pressure.

#### 4. Exterior Reveal
Once the player successfully brings Ronin outside, the Boss appears in a single decisive burst of motion.

The move should read as practiced authority, not flashy superhero nonsense.

Suggested image:

- the Boss drops into frame
- one foot, visibly non-human or partially transformed, pins Ronin by the neck
- it is not a killing blow
- it is dominance, restraint, and public correction

The reveal is not "surprise, the Boss is secretly a monster."

The reveal is:

**The Boss is licensed, connected, and fully at home in a social order the player only partly understood.**

#### 5. Institutional Confirmation
A passing police car does not meaningfully intervene.

This is important.

The authorities recognize that the Boss is acting within some accepted legal or quasi-legal authority.

Working term:

- `Lycanthropic Pack Authority`
- `LPA`

Each state or equivalent jurisdiction has an LPA office.

The Boss holds some kind of active restraint / enforcement credential through that system.

This moment sells the wider world:

- supernatural authority is bureaucratized
- violence can be licensed
- the city accepts ugly outcomes if the paperwork is in order

#### 6. Boss Demeanor Shift
After pinning Ronin, the Boss slowly steps off him without breaking eye contact, daring him to move.

Then the Boss looks to the player.

For a moment he still carries the same feral expression.
Then it flips into a calm, managerial smile.

He thanks the player and says he hopes to see them clock in tomorrow night.

That line should play as:

- appreciation
- recruitment
- normalization
- a mild threat

The subtext is that the player is now implicated in the real order of things.

#### 7. Ronin's Humiliation
The Boss grabs Ronin by the neck, hauls him bodily upward, and throws him into the cab or rear compartment of a large truck.

Ronin glares at the player the whole time.

This matters because the player did not defeat Ronin through force.
They changed his social position.

He now has to submit to the Boss.
That humiliation is what he carries out of the scene.

#### 8. Exit Beat
The scene fades out on the aftermath with a cool synthetic track, ideally something with hard-night-drive energy rather than sentimental triumph.

The mood should be:

- relief
- threat
- satisfaction
- contamination

The player survived and was useful.
They also learned what kind of workplace they actually joined.

### Tone Requirements
This ending should feel:

- competent
- ugly
- funny in a dry way
- socially revealing
- a little bit hot, frankly
- never sentimental

The Boss is not rescuing the player out of pure benevolence.
He is solving a problem, reasserting order, and making clear that he noticed the player's usefulness.

### What This Ending Reveals About The World
This ending should communicate several world truths very quickly:

- werewolves are normal enough to be administratively managed
- pack authority intersects with civil authority
- the Boss has a formal place in that system
- the bathhouse has been operating inside a tolerated zone of grime, risk, and controlled violence
- the player is now much closer to the real machinery of the setting

### Gameplay Requirements
For this ending to work, the game likely needs:

- a correct-identification route
- a final pursuit or pressure sequence
- a way to route or lure Ronin toward an exterior handoff zone
- an exterior staging area
- one or two earlier hints that the Boss has unusual authority
- one or two earlier hints that the city treats supernatural governance as regulated reality

### Do Not Lose
- The player must earn this ending by making the correct deduction.
- The Boss must not feel like a random deus ex machina.
- The reveal must reinforce the setting's institutional logic.
- The scene should end with the player feeling both validated and newly trapped.

### Open Questions
- What is the exact name of the Boss's LPA credential?
- How much of the Boss's transformed form is shown?
- Does Ronin speak during restraint, or does silence play better?
- Is the truck the Boss's personal vehicle, a bathhouse fleet vehicle, or an LPA-adjacent work truck?
- What exact earlier beats foreshadow the Boss's sanctioned authority?
